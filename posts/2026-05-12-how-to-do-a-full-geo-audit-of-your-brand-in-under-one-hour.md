## The 60-Minute GEO Audit Framework

### Phase 1: Map Your Current AI Visibility (15 minutes)

Start with manual queries across the big three AI platforms:

**ChatGPT 4.0:**
- Search for "[your industry] + best solutions"
- Try "[your main keyword] + recommendations" 
- Test "[competitor name] vs alternatives"

**Perplexity:**
- Query "top [your category] tools 2024"
- Search "[your use case] + best practices"
- Try "compare [competitor] alternatives"

**Google AI Overviews:**
- Search your primary keywords
- Test "[your city] + [your service]" (for local)
- Try buying intent queries like "best [product category]"

Document every mention. Or lack thereof.

Pro tip: Use incognito mode to avoid personalisation skewing results.

### Phase 2: Competitor Visibility Analysis (20 minutes)

Run the same queries but focus on competitor mentions. Create a simple tracking sheet:

| Platform | Query | Your Brand | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|----------|-------|------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| ChatGPT | "best CRM tools" | Not mentioned | Position 2 | Position 5 | Not mentioned |
| Perplexity | "CRM software comparison" | Not mentioned | Position 1 | Position 3 | Position 4 |
| Google AI | "top CRM platforms" | Position 7 | Position 2 | Position 1 | Not mentioned |

This reveals your competitive gap immediately.

### Phase 3: Content Source Audit (15 minutes)

When AI platforms mention competitors, they cite sources. Track these religiously.

Common citation sources we see:
- G2 and Capterra reviews
- Industry publication articles
- Company blog posts with strong SEO
- Third-party comparison sites
- Reddit discussions and forums

Check if your brand appears on these same sources. If competitors get cited from TechCrunch but you're not there, that's a gap.

### Phase 4: Query Expansion Testing (10 minutes)

Test variations of your core queries:
- Long-tail variations
- Problem-focused queries ("how to solve X")
- Comparison queries
- Local variations (if applicable)
- Industry-specific jargon

Different query types surface different results. Cast a wide net.

## Red Flags That Kill AI Search Visibility

During your audit, watch for these visibility killers:

**Inconsistent brand messaging** - AI models get confused when your positioning varies across sources.

**Thin review profiles** - Brands with under 50 G2/Capterra reviews rarely get AI mentions.

**Zero thought leadership content** - No industry publication mentions = no AI citations.

**Outdated information** - Old pricing, discontinued features, or stale company descriptions hurt relevance.

**Negative sentiment concentration** - A few bad reviews that dominate search results tank AI recommendations.
