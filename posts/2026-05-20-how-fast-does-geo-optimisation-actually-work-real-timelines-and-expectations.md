The question I get most from CMOs about Generative Engine Optimisation: "How long until we see results?"

Fair question. Traditional SEO takes 3-6 months. Paid search is instant but expensive. Where does GEO fit?

I've tracked optimization efforts across 50+ B2B brands over the past 18 months. Here's what the data actually shows about GEO timelines - not the marketing fluff, but real numbers from real campaigns.

## Week 1-2: The Foundation Phase

Don't expect miracles in week one. You're building the pipes, not seeing the water flow yet.

**What happens:**
- Content audit and gap analysis: 3-5 days
- Initial optimization implementations: 5-10 days  
- First baseline measurements in AI engines

**Realistic expectations:**
- Zero meaningful visibility changes
- ChatGPT mentions: Maybe 1-2% increase if you're lucky
- Perplexity citations: Usually flat
- Google AI Overviews: No movement

The one SaaS client who saw immediate results? They already had solid content - we just restructured it for AI consumption. That's the exception, not the rule.

## Week 3-4: First Green Shoots

This is where you start seeing actual movement. Not hockey stick growth, but genuine progress.

**Typical results from our tracking:**
- ChatGPT brand mentions: 15-25% increase
- Perplexity citations: 8-12% boost
- Google AI Overview appearances: 5-10% improvement

**Why the uptick?** AI models begin indexing your optimized content. The freshly structured FAQ sections, topic clusters, and entity-rich content start getting picked up.

Real example: A marketing automation company went from 3 ChatGPT mentions per week to 11 mentions by week 4. Not explosive, but directionally correct.

## Month 2: Momentum Builds

Month two is where GEO starts looking like a real channel, not just an experiment.

**Average improvements we're tracking:**
- Overall AI visibility: 35-50% increase from baseline
- Brand mention frequency: 2-3x weekly occurrences
- Topic authority signals: Clear improvement in niche queries

**The compounding effect kicks in.** Your optimized content starts cross-referencing itself. AI engines begin treating you as a legitimate source in your category.

One ecommerce brand hit 74% AI visibility increase in month two. But they were starting from near-zero, pumping out optimized content daily, and had budget for aggressive implementation.

## Month 3: The Inflection Point

This is where GEO either takes off or plateaus. The difference? How systematically you're approaching optimization.

**High performers (top 25% of brands we track):**
- 80-120% AI visibility increase
- Consistent daily mentions across all three major AI platforms
- Beginning to rank for competitor comparison queries

**Average performers:**
- 40-60% improvement  
- Sporadic but regular mentions
- Good coverage for branded queries, limited progress on category terms

**What separates them?** The high performers treat GEO like performance marketing. They're measuring, iterating, and optimizing weekly. The average performers set it and forget it.

## Month 4-6: Scaling Phase

By month four, you know if your GEO strategy is working. The data is clear.

**Successful campaigns typically show:**

## Factors That Accelerate GEO Results

**Content volume matters more than you think.** Brands publishing 3+ optimized pieces weekly see results 40% faster than those publishing weekly.

**Starting visibility level is crucial.** If you're already getting some AI mentions, optimization works faster. Starting from absolute zero takes longer to gain momentum.

**Technical implementation speed.** The faster you can implement structured data, entity markup, and content formatting, the faster AI engines recognize and reward the changes.

**Category competition.** B2B SaaS sees results faster than ecommerce. Less noise, clearer authority signals.

## What Slows Down GEO Progress

**Treating it like traditional SEO.** AI engines value different signals. Keyword density doesn't matter. Entity relationships and context do.

**Inconsistent optimization.** Publishing one great piece monthly won't cut it. AI engines reward consistent, systematic content improvement.

**Ignoring technical basics.** Schema markup, clean content structure, and fast loading speeds aren't optional for GEO.

**Not measuring properly.** You can't optimize what you don't track. Most brands are flying blind on AI visibility.

## FAQ: GEO Timeline Questions

**How long until ChatGPT starts mentioning my brand regularly?**

For most B2B brands with decent existing content, 4-6 weeks for sporadic mentions, 8-10 weeks for regular mentions. Starting from zero? Add 2-4 weeks to those timelines.

**Can I speed up GEO results with more budget?**

Yes, but there are diminishing returns. Doubling content production can cut timeline by 25-30%. But you can't buy your way to instant AI authority like you can with paid ads.

**Why is my competitor ranking in AI results but not me?**

Usually comes down to content structure and entity optimization. They're probably not "better" - just better formatted for AI consumption. This gap typically takes 6-8 weeks to close with systematic optimization.

**Should I focus on ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI first?**

Google AI Overviews for immediate traffic impact. ChatGPT for brand awareness. Perplexity for thought leadership. But honestly, optimize for all three simultaneously - the technical requirements overlap significantly.

**How do I know if my GEO timeline is on track?**

You should see some measurable improvement by week 3-4. If you're not seeing any movement by week 6, either your baseline measurement is wrong or your optimization approach needs fixing.

The bottom line on GEO timelines? It's faster than traditional SEO but slower than paid search. Budget 3-4 months to see meaningful results, 6+ months to build sustainable AI visibility.

Most importantly: start measuring now. You can't optimize what you don't track, and the brands that started tracking AI visibility six months ago have a massive head start.
