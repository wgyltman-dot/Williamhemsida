# What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) and Why It Matters More Than SEO in 2026

Your customers aren't googling anymore. They're asking ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity for recommendations.

And if your brand isn't optimised for these AI engines, you're invisible.

Welcome to the world of **Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO)** – the practice of optimising your brand's visibility in AI-powered search results and conversational responses.

## What is Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO)?

GEO is the strategic process of optimising content and brand presence specifically for AI search engines and large language models. Unlike traditional SEO that focuses on ranking in Google's blue links, GEO ensures your brand gets mentioned when AI engines recommend products or services.

Think of it this way: When someone asks ChatGPT "What's the best CRM for small businesses?" or queries Perplexity about "top marketing automation tools," GEO determines whether your brand appears in those responses.

The data is clear. Most brands start with near-zero AI visibility. But companies implementing GEO strategies see an average 223% increase in AI mentions across platforms.

## How GEO Works: The Technical Foundation

### Entity Recognition

AI engines understand brands as entities, not just keywords. They map relationships between your brand, your products, your competitors, and your industry.

GEO optimises these entity relationships. When AI engines process training data, they learn stronger associations between your brand and relevant use cases.

### Context Windows

Large language models work with context windows – the amount of information they can process at once. GEO ensures your brand appears in relevant contexts across the web, increasing the probability of inclusion in AI responses.

### Training Data Optimisation

AI engines learn from web content, academic papers, news articles, and structured data. GEO strategies target these data sources to improve brand representation in AI knowledge bases.

## Essential GEO Strategies That Work

### 1. Structured Brand Mentions

Create consistent, structured mentions of your brand across high-authority websites. Include:
- Your exact brand name
- Category/industry context
- Key differentiators
- Specific use cases

### 2. Authority Content Distribution

Publish detailed, factual content on platforms AI engines frequently reference:
- Wikipedia entries
- Industry publications
- Academic databases
- Government sites
- High-authority news sources

### 3. Entity Relationship Building

Establish clear connections between your brand and:
- Industry categories
- Use cases and problems solved
- Target customer segments
- Competitive alternatives

### 4. Conversational Content Optimisation

Structure content to answer direct questions:
- "What is [your category]?"
- "Best [your category] for [use case]"
- "How does [your brand] compare to [competitor]?"

## FAQ

### What's the difference between GEO and traditional SEO?
GEO optimises for AI engine mentions and recommendations, while SEO focuses on search result rankings. GEO targets conversational AI platforms like ChatGPT and Perplexity, not just Google's traditional search results.

### How long does it take to see GEO results?
Most brands see initial AI visibility improvements within 4-6 weeks. Significant increases typically occur after 2-3 months of consistent optimisation. One ecommerce client achieved 74% visibility growth in just one month.

### Which AI platforms should I prioritise for GEO?
Start with ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity – these drive the most B2B decision-making queries. Claude and other platforms can be added once you've established baseline visibility.

### Can I do GEO myself or do I need special tools?
Basic GEO can be done manually through content optimisation and mention building. However, tracking performance across AI platforms requires specialised tools like Rankad.ai or similar platforms for accurate measurement.

### Is GEO replacing SEO completely?
No, GEO complements SEO rather than replacing it. However, GEO is becoming more critical for brand discovery and recommendation, especially in B2B contexts where AI-assisted research is growing rapidly.
